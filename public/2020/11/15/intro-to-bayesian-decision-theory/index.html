Intro to Bayesian Decision Theory

2020-11-15

As the pilot of my new blog site, I would like to touch on Bayesian decision theory, which can be found in many statistical inference textbooks, such as Shao (2003).

Decision Theory

Under the framework of decision theory, we define \(X \in \mathcal{X}\) as observed random variables (or data), \(\theta \in \Theta\) as state of nature (or parameter of the data generator), and \(a \in \mathcal{A}\) as action to be taken.

A decision rule \(T: \mathcal{X} \mapsto \mathcal{A}\) is a function that takes action \(a = T(X)\) upon observing \(X\). To evaluate an action, we define loss function \(L: (\Theta \times \mathcal{A}) \mapsto \mathbb{R}\). Naturally, a risk function is used to evaluate decision rules: \[R(\theta, T) = \operatorname{E}\left[L\left(\theta, T(X)\right)\, \Big\vert\, \theta\right]\]

Shao, Jun. 2003. Mathematical Statistics. 2nd ed. Springer Texts in Statistics. New York: Springer.
